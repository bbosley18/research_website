Currently I am completing my senior year as a Posse Foundation Scholar at the College of Wooster in Wooster, Ohio. Upon graduating from the College of Wooster as a History major and Comptuer Science minor I hope to pursue a Ph.D. in History.

I was always fascinated with history, and homeschooling enabled me to spend copious amounts of time diving deeper into historical topics that were broached in my history textbooks. However, science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) were also integrated into my education. In fact, I initially arrived at the College of Wooster, as a Posse Foundation scholar prepared to pursue a STEM-related degree. However, when I enrolled in a required First Year Seminar conducted by the History Department, I discovered my passion for history. The next year, I declared a history major, with a minor in computer science. By combining my passion for history and computer science, I wanted my research to revolve around the history of minority groups and utilize technology as a platform for historical interpretation.
I decided that when given the opportunity to do historical research I would integrate technology into my projects.
As an African American woman, I have always been interested in the history of black women and children. By studying this subject I hope to share the impactful changes made by these minorities within society. Thus, my senior research project focuses on the experiences of black youth in the south during Reconstruction to the start of Jim Crow. Furthermore, I am utilizing the slave narratives from the Freedman's Bureau archive to create a set of animated interviews that can be experienced through the HTC Vive. Through my research, I hope to introduce diverse points of view and stories of the most vulnerable demographic to the public through technology.
