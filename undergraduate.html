Computer Science- Junior Thesis

As early as the 19th century, indigenous people were being taken from their lands by westerners and placed in exhibits like circuses, world fairs, and in the case of this project, ethnographic exhibits, also known as human zoos. At human zoos, the indigenous people were put inside of an environment that replicates their home; while being forced to practice their traditions. Thus, making them a source of edu-cation for Westerners on the variety of colonized cultures; it also ingrained a racial superiority within the Westerners who visited these exhibitions. Although human zoos lasted until the late 20th century, the history of these exhibits have gone untold for decades; however, through this project technology will create a way to educate people on untold stories. In addition, this project will also allow for the anima-tion of the human zoo to be used as a tool for some in the future to teach about tragic events. This project uses Unity which is a software that allows for people to build virtual reality, 2D, and 3D worlds. This project uses many of the features of Unity to create an immersive environment that is user friendly and educational. The features used in this project are images, audio, and videos which help to convey to the audience the importance of this historical time. Thus, this project engages with the user and the material in a different way.

History - Junior Thesis

Names like Malcom X, Nat Turner, Dr. King, and many others have always been used as a means of educating the black community about the many activist who have helped to advance the black community. However, the only activist that we have learned about have been men. In fact, it seems as if the black female activist has been erased from the history of the black community. Through my Junior IS I hope to find sources that will bring to light the hidden stories of Black women who sacrificed themselves to uplift their community.

History - Senior Thesis

When we try to imagine slavery and the emancipation era in the United States, the experiences and activism of black children rarely come to mind. This IS focuses on the lived experiences of black youth within the period of 1865-1914 and looks specifically at topics such as slavery, the Civil War, Reconstruction, and the making of Jim Crow. Additionally, as a digital IS I hope that my audience can fully understand the challenges that black youth faced during the period through a virtual museum.
